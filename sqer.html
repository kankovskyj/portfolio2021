SQER

App design

Background

SQER was the second app I designed from it’s inception, but my first in the fintech/social sphere. I was responsible for the initial research, designs and even played a part in new features that were implemented.

The project

At its core, SQER is a tool for tracking and paying for shared expenses. If that sounds and looks simple enough, then I was successful in what I set out to do. With complex math operations, analytics that serve users to get the most of tracking their expenses or auto-generated elements, aligning an easy to use interface with the app's complexities was the main challenge.

Design

I chose a user-first approach to design, and along with social media-like features and interactions, I achieved a non-formal and friendly vibe that quickly breaks any stigma associated with debt tracking while providing an extremely useful tool to users.

Development

Since I consulted the design and my progress overall pretty much on a daily basis, we were able to smoothly transition from wireframes to code. I also developed a micro-site for the app with a cool twist, and the app is now waiting for approval from Apple.

What I achieved

I’ve utilized the full extent of my skillset ranging from user research, wireframes, and testing all the way to coding a microsite. After extensive user testing in collaboration with third-party users and friends, we developed an app that is ahead of the competition in its sector thanks to its usability and smooth user experience.
